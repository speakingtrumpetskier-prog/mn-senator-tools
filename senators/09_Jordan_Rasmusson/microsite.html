The Investment Record — Otter Tail County
A Facility Built for Youth in Crisis

Rasmusson chief-authored SF917 to request $1.05 million in state bonding for a 12-unit multipurpose youth transitional facility in Perham.

The facility serves youth ages 15 to 21 who face substance use, mental health challenges, or housing instability. It combines residential beds with wraparound support services in a single location.

Otter Tail County has one of the largest land areas in Minnesota. Services are spread thin. This facility puts help where it is needed.

Recovery Housing That Actually Exists

Through the Human Services budget, Rasmusson secured $143,000 per year for transitional recovery housing in Otter Tail County. Over four years, that totals $572,000.

The funding supports three named facilities: Perham 180, Matthew House, and Barbara's House. Each provides room, board, and services for adults recovering from substance use disorder, mental illness, or housing instability.

These are not abstract programs. They are buildings with doors in communities where people know each other.

The Accusation
Rasmusson sits as the top Republican on the Human Services Committee that oversees health care access for Minnesotans. In January 2025, he authored SF690 to strip every undocumented person in Minnesota of every state-funded service at once, including health insurance and college scholarships. The bill does not narrow a program. It eliminates eligibility in a single sweep.

SF 690 — Bill Summary (2025–2026 Session)
Referred: State & Local Government  •  Chief Author: Rasmusson
SEC. 1. PROHIBITION ON STATE-FUNDED SERVICES.
 
A state agency, political subdivision, or public institution
must not provide any service, payment, grant, loan, subsidy,
or other financial assistance to an individual who is not
lawfully present in the United States.
 
SEC. 2. MINNESOTACARE ELIGIBILITY.
 
Undocumented noncitizens are ineligible for MinnesotaCare.
Eligibility is limited to citizens, nationals, and lawfully
present noncitizens only.
 
SEC. 3. NORTH STAR PROMISE SCHOLARSHIP.
 
Undocumented noncitizens are explicitly ineligible for the
North Star Promise scholarship program.
 
Introduced: January 27, 2025. Chief Author: Sen. Jordan Rasmusson (R-09).
Cosponsors: Gruenhagen, Dornink, Drazkowski, Koran.

Evidence — Three Cards (Hover to Examine)
Health Access
17,396
People Enrolled in MinnesotaCare
As of April 2025, 17,396 undocumented people were enrolled in MinnesotaCare statewide. That is more than twice the state's original forecast of 7,700. SF690 would have ended coverage for all of them.[2]
College Access
SF 690
North Star Promise Shutdown
The North Star Promise is Minnesota's free-college program for residents under an income threshold. Undocumented students apply via the Minnesota Dream Act. SF690 would have barred them entirely, with no grandfather provision.[3]
Committee Position
No. 1
Republican on Human Services
Rasmusson is the top Republican on the Senate Human Services Committee. The committee has jurisdiction over MinnesotaCare and the programs SF690 targets. He authored a cutoff bill for programs he oversees.[4]
What He Says
"We face cuts to special education, nursing homes, and disability services." He frames SF690 as protecting existing beneficiaries from budget competition.
What SF 690 Actually Does
SF690 would impose a sweeping cutoff on state-tax-funded aid for undocumented or otherwise not lawfully present noncitizens. It does not redirect savings to nursing homes, special education, or disability services. Those programs have no guaranteed benefit from the bill. The trade-off he describes does not exist in the legislation.

What that means for lawmakers.

Urgency. There was a narrative last year that AI had hit a wall in capabilities. It was a plausible perspective at the time. It isn't anymore. After breakthroughs late last year, AI systems' ability to automate cyberattacks, pathogen design, misinformation, mass surveillance, and white-collar work is advancing faster than ever.

The world will soon change faster than the typical pace of policymaking. The only solution is to act with atypical speed: AI regulations, data center moratoriums, calling for a federal pause on AI development, worker displacement funds.
