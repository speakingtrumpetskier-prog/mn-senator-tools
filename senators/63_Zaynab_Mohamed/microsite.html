What the law does

Contract for deed was a trap.
She closed it.

Contract for deed is a financing arrangement where a buyer makes payments directly to a seller and receives the deed only after full payment. In Minnesota, investors had learned to exploit this structure. They would sell a home on contract, pocket payments for months or years, then terminate the deal and keep both the property and the money.

Mohamed's district felt this directly. South Minneapolis has a high share of renter and buyer households from immigrant communities. Those communities were disproportionately targeted by investor sellers running this cycle repeatedly on the same properties.

HF1792 rewrote the statutory definition of "investor seller" and tightened the anti-churning rules. Investor sellers are now subject to stronger disclosure rules, including purchase-price and acquisition-date disclosures tied to a two-year lookback. Buyers can verify more of a property's history before signing.

HF1792 — Key Statutory Change

The 2025 law adds a stronger anti-churning framework for investor sellers, including a 90-day notice standard for cancellations and tighter rules around repeatedly terminating and re-selling the same property on contract for deed.

The law also keeps investor sellers responsible for real estate taxes on the property and expands the disclosure package buyers receive before signing.

Investor Seller — New Definition

The statute builds a broader investor-seller framework aimed at repeat sellers and quick-turn resales. Required disclosures include purchase price, acquisition date, and prior contract history.

How the law works
01
Anti-Churning Rule

The law tightened anti-churning rules for investor sellers, making repeat terminations and quick re-sales harder to recycle into another exploitative contract-for-deed deal.

02
Mandatory Disclosure

Investor sellers must disclose what they paid for the property and when. Buyers can verify they are not overpaying to someone who flipped the same deed multiple times.

03
90-Day Notice Rule

Investor-seller contract cancellations now carry a 90-day notice standard. That gives buyers more time and clearer legal footing before losing the property interest they have been paying into.

South Minneapolis • District 63

District 63 is where this story starts.

South Minneapolis is younger, more renter-heavy, and more economically precarious than most districts in the state. Housing cost and housing security are not abstract issues here. They determine whether a family builds equity or loses it.

Mohamed came into this seat with direct knowledge of how predatory contract-for-deed deals worked in her community. She served as a Senior Policy Aide at the City of Minneapolis before her election. She knew where the statutory gaps were.

HF1792 is the result. It passed. It is law. Mohamed was the Senate chief author of the companion bill, SF505, and the enacted statute gives buyers clearer disclosure rights, stronger anti-churning protections, and a longer cancellation notice standard.

Sequence of events
STEP 01 — BILL INTRODUCTION
SF2621 Introduced — March 2025

SF2621 was introduced in the Senate on March 17, 2025 with Mohamed as chief author. The bill proposes a constitutional amendment to raise the state sales tax by three-eighths of one percent and dedicate the receipts to housing purposes.

STEP 02 — FISCAL CLAIM
Constitutional Tax Increase for Housing Funds

The bill would increase the state sales tax rate beginning July 1, 2027 and create the homeownership opportunity fund, community and household stability fund, and rental opportunity fund. Because it is written as a constitutional amendment, it would require voter approval and could not be undone by ordinary legislation alone.

STEP 03 — CONTEXT
The Proposal Advanced in 2026

SF2621 did not die on introduction. It moved through Housing and Homelessness Prevention, then Health and Human Services, then State and Local Government, and was re-referred to Taxes in March 2026. Mohamed's housing politics are real. So is her willingness to pair them with a statewide constitutional tax increase.
