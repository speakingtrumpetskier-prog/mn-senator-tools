The Record
SF 2884 — 94th Legislature — Chief Author: Frentz

Omnibus Pension and Retirement Reform Act, 2025

Frentz chaired the Legislative Commission on Pensions and Retirement and carried the omnibus pension bill for the 2025 session. SF 2884 became 2025 Session Law Chapter 37. The bill covered four separate retirement systems. Each fix addressed a specific gap in the formula, not a generic benefit increase.

For teachers, the early retirement window widened. The minimum age for enhanced early retirement dropped from 62 to 60, and the reduction penalty dropped from 6% to 5%. Teachers who spent careers in Mankato-area classrooms can now retire two years sooner at better terms.4

For police and firefighters, the COLA wait dropped from three years to two after retirement, and every retiree received a one-time compounded 3% increase in calendar year 2026.5

TRA: Early retirement age 62 → 60
TRA: Early retirement penalty 6% → 5%
PERA P&F: First COLA wait 3 yrs → 2 yrs
PERA P&F: One-time 2026 COLA +3% compounded
State Patrol: Annual COLA 1.0% → 1.25%

Nick A. Frentz
Senate District 18 — DFL — North Mankato — Attorney

Frentz is a former attorney and institutional Democrat who chairs the Senate Energy, Utilities, Environment, and Climate Committee and serves as Assistant Majority Leader. He is also Chair of the Legislative Commission on Pensions and Retirement. His brand is measured competence and large negotiated packages. His sports betting campaign sits uneasily inside that brand.

Evidence
EXHIBIT 01 — SF 0757

Added His Name to the Original Betting Bill

Frentz was added as a chief author on SF 0757 on March 27, 2025. The bill authorized sports betting and fantasy contests with full tax structure and criminal penalties. It failed on a 6–6 committee vote. Two DFL senators crossed the aisle to kill it.3

SF 0757 — Chief Author: Klein; Frentz added 3/27/2025
EXHIBIT 02 — SF 3414

Filed a Second Betting Bill in the Same Session

When SF 0757 stalled, Frentz co-authored SF 3414 with Sen. Matt Klein. The revised bill proposed up to 11 tribal operator licenses valid for 20 years, a 22% tax on net revenue, and wagers available to anyone 21 or older in Minnesota. The bill also died without reaching the floor.4

SF 3414 — Co-authors: Frentz, Klein — April 2025
EXHIBIT 03 — Opposition Record

His Own Colleagues Blocked It on Addiction Grounds

Sen. Jon Marty (DFL-Roseville) proposed directing 50% of sports betting revenue to addiction treatment, compared to the 10% earmark in SF 3414. Religious groups and advocates for problem gamblers testified against the expansion. The Senate committee split 6–6. The bill never moved again.5

Committee vote: 6–6 — Two DFLers voted no
