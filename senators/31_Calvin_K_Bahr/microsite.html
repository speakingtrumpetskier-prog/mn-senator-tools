SF2145 • Chief Author: Sen. Calvin K. Bahr • 93rd Legislature

TH-65 / Anoka County Road 60 Intersection Safety Project — Ham Lake

Highway 65 through Ham Lake is not a bureaucratic abstraction. It is the road that District 31 drivers take to work every morning. For years it was one of the most dangerous stretches in the state. Bahr filed a $2,273,000 state bond request to redesign the intersection at Trunk Highway 65 and Anoka County Road 60.

The project realigns the frontage and backage roads on both sides of the intersection. That redesign targets the conflict points that caused crashes. It is targeted, local, and engineered as a specific capital request.

The Sequence of Events

2020–21
House, 91st Leg.

20 Failed Votes to End Governor's Emergency Powers

As a House member, Bahr joined Republican efforts to strip Governor Walz of emergency authority during COVID-19. House Republicans tried to end the peacetime emergency 20 times in 14 months. Every attempt failed. The pattern was set: oppose executive emergency response as a matter of ideology.

2023
93rd Legislature

Bahr Moves to Senate, Reintroduces Emergency Restrictions

Elected to the Senate in 2022, Bahr brought the same emergency-powers fight with him. He chief-authored SF0454 in the 93rd Legislature, seeking to require legislative approval before any declared emergency could extend past five days and to deny executive orders the force of law unless the Legislature enacted them within that window.

2024
June & Aug.

Real Emergencies Required Fast Executive Action

Governor Walz issued Emergency Executive Order 24-05 to deploy the Minnesota National Guard for flood response after heavy rainfall across southern Minnesota. Separately, tornado damage struck Aitkin, Crow Wing, Beltrami, Big Stone, and Todd counties, requiring state disaster assistance coordination within days. Under Bahr's bill, each of those deployments would require a legislative vote before the order could remain in effect past day five.

2025
94th Legislature

Bahr Reintroduces the Same Bill Again

Bahr filed SF454 again in the 94th Legislature. The bill requires legislative approval to extend a declared emergency beyond five days and requires legislative enactment before executive orders and rules may have the force of law. The bill was co-sponsored by Drazkowski, Mathews, and Lucero. It has not become law. But Bahr is committed to it.

Core Document • SF454 • 94th Legislature, 2025–2026
What the Bill Actually Does

SF454, as authored by Bahr, does two things. First, it requires a legislative vote to extend any declared emergency past five days. Under existing Minnesota law, the governor may extend an emergency by Executive Council resolution for up to 30 days. Bahr's bill strips that flexibility. Second, it requires the Legislature to enact any executive order or rule within five days or it loses the force of law.

The Legislature is not always in session. Calling a special session takes time. Disasters do not wait for a quorum. In 2024 alone, Minnesota required emergency response for flood events in southern counties, tornado damage across six counties, and major storm events. Each of those situations required swift, unilateral executive coordination that Bahr's bill would have placed on hold.
