Project Brief — SF 2138 — Water Infrastructure

The 25-year-old plant
cannot keep up.

The Joint Powers Water Board serves Albertville, Hanover, and St. Michael. Its treatment facility is already running at peak capacity on high-demand days. In a dry year like 2024, staff faced the prospect of blending untreated water into the regular supply. That is not an abstract risk. It is a documented operational constraint at a plant built for a smaller district.

The source water carries naturally occurring manganese and radium at levels that exceed state safety thresholds. Manganese in the district's groundwater has tested at up to 15 times the Minnesota Department of Health's recommended limit for infants and 5 times the adult limit. The current system cannot filter it adequately at scale.

Lucero chief-authored SF 2138 to seek $10 million in state bonding funds for the expansion. As introduced, the bill would fund improvements necessary to expand treatment capacity from ten to 15 million gallons and address manganese and radium water-quality issues.

This is not a policy proposal. It is a social media talking point converted into a legislative filing. The bill takes a Twitter-era insult and attempts to inscribe it into Minnesota's mental health statutes.

The American Psychiatric Association does not recognize "Trump Derangement Syndrome" as a condition. No medical or diagnostic literature supports the definition in this bill. Lucero introduced it anyway.

The political problem

District 30 is also dealing with a regional water-system request. The contrast here is between that introduced infrastructure bill and a separate introduced bill about "Trump Derangement Syndrome."

Pattern, not anomaly
