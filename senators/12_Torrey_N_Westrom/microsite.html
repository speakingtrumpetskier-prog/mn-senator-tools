Key Pro Fact

SF4539: Sauk Centre Gets
Its Highway Money

Sauk Centre authorized a half-cent local sales tax in 2019 to fund reconstruction of Trunk Highway 71 through town. The original cap was $10 million. Construction costs moved. The city needed more room.

Westrom chief-authored SF4539 in the 94th Legislature to let Sauk Centre put that question to voters. The bill would raise the bonding authority to $20 million and extend the tax through December 31, 2045 — or until the city collects its $20 million, whichever comes first.

MnDOT's official Sauk Centre project materials estimate the TH 71 work at $26.4 million. MnDOT's 2026 project notice says reconstruction is planned for 2028–2029. The work includes roadway, utility, and pedestrian improvements. SF4539 is Westrom's bill to let Sauk Centre ask voters for a higher local sales-tax ceiling to help cover the local share.

This is legislative work that does not generate press releases. It generates pavement.

District Context
District 12 runs from Traverse County on the North Dakota line east through Stearns County farm country. For every town in this corridor, "infrastructure" means the main-street highway that every grain truck and school bus uses. TH 71 is not abstract policy. It is Sauk Centre's downtown.
Why This Matters
Local sales tax bills require the city to go back to voters. The legislature's job is to set the ceiling. Without SF4539, Sauk Centre could not ask voters for what the project actually costs. Westrom cleared that path.
