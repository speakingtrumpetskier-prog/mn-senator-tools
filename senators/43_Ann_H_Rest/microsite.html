Rest's latest bill would turn Legislative Auditor recommendations into agency management rules

SF3900 is a management-and-oversight bill. As engrossed, it would require regular guidance to executive agencies on implementing Office of the Legislative Auditor recommendations.

It also would require annual inventory training for state employees responsible for state property and reporting when uncollectible debt reaches $100,000 or more. Those are not slogans. They are the fine-print controls that determine whether audit findings actually change behavior.

The Senate record shows SF3900 was introduced on February 26, 2026, amended, and reported out of committee on March 18 to Judiciary and Public Safety. That is a real oversight bill with a real paper trail.

District record

Forty years of fiscal work in the northwest suburbs

Rest represents Crystal, Golden Valley, New Hope, Robbinsdale, and a ward of Plymouth. These are inner-ring suburbs with strong civic traditions, established institutions, and voters who read the fine print.

Her official Senate record lists her as President Pro Tem, chair of the Taxes Committee, and vice chair of Rules and Administration. The Legislative Audit Commission lists her as current chair for 2025-2026 and as a past chair in five earlier bienniums.

Rest is a CPA by training. SF3900 fits that background. It is not a ribbon cutting. It is a bill about agency follow-through after audits, inventory controls, and debt reporting.

In September 2025, Rest announced she will not seek re-election in 2026. That makes SF3900 read less like campaign copy and more like a last-pass process bill from a legislator who has spent decades inside the machinery.

What this means

SF3197 would tax social media companies based on how many Minnesota consumers they reach. The bill sets one rate over 100,000 Minnesota consumers, a higher monthly formula over 500,000, and a still higher one over 1,000,000.

That is not a tax on profits. It is a gross receipts design keyed to audience scale. The policy choice is straightforward: Rest kept looking for new ways to tax the digital economy.

Then in 2026 she also joined SF4787, which would add sales tax to advertising services defined broadly enough to include search engine marketing, lead generation optimization, buying internet ad space, and website traffic evaluation.
