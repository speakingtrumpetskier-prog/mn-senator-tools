The Problem
Manganese
Manganese in groundwater poses neurological risks, especially for infants and children. Howard Lake's aging well infrastructure has not been upgraded to address it.
What SF 2582 Funds
New well & plant
A new water treatment facility, a new municipal well, trunk water-main upgrades, and water tower improvements near the site.1
Who Benefits
Howard Lake
Residents, businesses, and school-age children who drink tap water in this fast-growing Wright County city.

Howard Lake sits in the middle of Wright County's growth corridor. New families are moving in. Old pipes are staying put. A first-term senator from Buffalo filed a bill to close that gap in his first legislative session.

SF 2582 requests $15 million in state bonding to build a new water treatment plant capable of removing manganese from municipal drinking water. The bill also funds trunk water-main upgrades and improvements to the well and tower near the facility site.

This is basic-service infrastructure. It does not require complex policy arguments. It requires a legislator willing to put a local number in front of the Capital Investment Committee and make the case for it.

Holmstrom ran as a small business owner. He campaigned on local responsiveness and practical governance. SF 4311 does the opposite for small Medicaid providers in his own district. It gives a state commissioner the power to shut down billing â€” the financial lifeline of every provider practice â€” without an administrative appeal after the bill's notice-and-cure process, and in a separate compliance clause.

The bill does include notice and cure periods before one type of suspension, but it still pairs a 60-day termination notice with immediate suspension of billing once the cure window expires. It also adds enrollment fees on covered provider types through the existing application-fee framework.

Holmstrom was not naive about the context. He entered the Senate while the provider crisis was front-page news. He sat on the Judiciary Committee. He chose to file this bill in March 2026 with the existing damage fully documented.
