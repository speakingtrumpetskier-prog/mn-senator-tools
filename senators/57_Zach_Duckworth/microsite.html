The Case for District 57

A fast-growing city needs water to drink and firefighters with places to work. Duckworth filed the Lakeville request for both.

Lakeville's official Census estimate rose from 69,490 on April 1, 2020 to 77,971 on July 1, 2024. Growth that fast puts pressure on water systems and public safety capacity. District 57 needed a senator willing to file a district-specific bonding request.

Duckworth chief-authored SF24, a 2025 first special-session bill requesting $5.4 million for Lakeville property acquisition for drinking-water treatment and a fire station site. The request is concrete even though it was not enacted in that form.1

Core Document
SF3079 — BILL SUMMARY Sole Author: Sen. Zach Duckworth, District 57

What the bill does, in plain language

SF3079 does one clear thing. It would narrow North Star Promise so the scholarship applies only to students enrolled in programs the state classifies as leading to high-demand industries and occupations.

Description:
Limits North Star Promise scholarship eligibility to students enrolled in programs in high-demand industries and occupations.

Action:
Introduced March 27, 2025 and referred to Higher Education.

Final action note:
See First Special Session, SF1.

The political choice is the story. Duckworth did not file a broad access bill. He filed a narrowing bill that would push state scholarship aid toward programs the state labels as workforce priorities.

Lakeville Public Schools serves more than 12,000 students. Duckworth chaired that district's school board before filing this higher-education restriction.3,4

Three Points of Record
Evidence 01 • Scholarship Filter

He moved to narrow a statewide last-dollar scholarship

North Star Promise was built as a statewide tuition-support program. Duckworth filed a bill that would make eligibility contingent on a student being enrolled in a program tied to high-demand industries and occupations.

Source: SF3079 status page and bill description
Evidence 02 • Sole Author

This was his bill, not a stray coauthor line

The Revisor status page lists Duckworth as the only author. The bill was introduced on March 27, 2025, referred to Higher Education, and later folded into first special-session negotiations through SF1.

Source: SF3079 authors and actions
Evidence 03 • The Credential

He ran Lakeville schools before filing a statewide college-choice restriction

Duckworth's school-board chairmanship is listed as a qualification for office. It is also the context for reading SF3079. He used education credibility to argue that some college and career paths should qualify for state aid and others should not.

Source: Minnesota Senate biography and SF3079 record

What that means for lawmakers.

Urgency. There was a narrative last year that AI had hit a wall in capabilities. It was a plausible perspective at the time. It isn't anymore. After breakthroughs late last year, AI systems' ability to automate cyberattacks, pathogen design, misinformation, mass surveillance, and white-collar work is advancing faster than ever.

The world will soon change faster than the typical pace of policymaking. The only solution is to act with atypical speed: AI regulations, data center moratoriums, calling for a federal pause on AI development, worker displacement funds.
