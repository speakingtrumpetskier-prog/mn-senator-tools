The Dam Problem

Why This Structure Matters

SF3773 does not spread money across a broad program. It identifies one dam, one county, one funding source, and one requested dollar amount.

In 2026, Wesenberg co-authored SF3773 to seek a $500,000 Environment and Natural Resources Trust Fund appropriation for replacement of the Sullivan Lake Dam in Morrison County.

That makes the case for him as a legislator willing to put a named district project into bill text, while avoiding any claim that the replacement was already funded or completed.

Sequence of Events
01
Minnesota Expands Coverage
In 2023, Minnesota enacted the Protect Reproductive Options Act, establishing abortion as a fundamental right under state law. Separate follow-on legislation and insurance changes confirmed Medical Assistance and MinnesotaCare coverage and required individual and small-group plans to include abortion as a covered benefit effective January 2025.
Source: MN Health Law (2023); MN Stat. § 62Q.5241
02
Wesenberg Files SF3775
On February 23, 2026, Wesenberg chief-authored SF3775, a bill to strip abortion coverage from Medical Assistance, MinnesotaCare, the State Employees Group Insurance Program, and to remove abortion as a mandatory benefit for most health plans. The bill was referred to the Senate Commerce and Consumer Protection Committee.
Source: SF3775, MN 94th Legislature, February 23, 20262
03
The Rural Gap Widens
District 10 covers Aitkin, Benton, Crow Wing, Isanti, Kanabec, Mille Lacs, and Morrison county territory. CHI St. Gabriel's Health in Little Falls is one hospital in the district. Specialized reproductive health providers are still concentrated farther south, including in the Twin Cities metro. Stripping insurance coverage does not eliminate the need. It eliminates the means to pay.
Source: Morrison County Health & Human Services; CHI St. Gabriel's Health3
Evidence Cards — SF3775
Bill Language
This Is Not a Conscience Clause
SF3775 does not allow providers to opt out. It does not regulate notification. It removes abortion from the list of covered services across four program categories. Patients who had coverage under Medical Assistance will no longer have coverage. The bill uses the word "prohibit."
Source: SF3775 bill text, MN Revisor of Statutes2
Brand Conflict
The Biologist Who Cut Health Benefits
Wesenberg presents as an outdoors and environment senator — a DNR-trained biologist focused on water quality, wildlife, and public land. SF3775 is his most significant authored bill of the 2026 session. It is a direct rollback of health coverage for tens of thousands of Minnesotans, co-authored by someone whose public brand is conservation.
Source: MN Senate bill file; MN Senate Republicans biography4
District Consequence
Rural Patients Pay the Price
District 10 is a large rural district with scattered providers and long drive times for specialty care. Low-income patients in the district who rely on Medical Assistance or MinnesotaCare have no obvious coverage substitute if SF3775 passes. The cost of care does not disappear. It shifts to patients who cannot absorb it.
Source: Morrison County HHS; CHI St. Gabriel's Health; MN Rural Health Hub3

What that means for lawmakers.

The only solution is to act with atypical speed: AI regulations, data center moratoriums, calling for a federal pause on AI development, worker displacement funds.
