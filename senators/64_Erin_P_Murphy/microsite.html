The Key Investment

A grant program built for these blocks

What the CORE Grant does on the ground

The Capitol Outreach for Revitalization & Expansion Grant is administered by the Saint Paul Port Authority in partnership with the State of Minnesota. It targets exactly the kind of small, for-profit businesses that line the District 64 corridor—storefronts that need a facade renovation, a new piece of equipment, or an accessibility ramp they cannot afford alone.

Grants run between $50,000 and $150,000. Eligible projects include facade enhancements, energy upgrades, new equipment, accessibility improvements, and building safety upgrades. Round One opened January 2026 with an application deadline of March 31, 2026.

This is not a housing voucher or a statewide formula. The money is geographically specific. It was written for the Capitol corridor—Murphy’s home turf—and it reaches businesses that would not qualify for standard commercial lending.

The Record

A nurse who legislates like one

Healthcare, staffing, and keeping Minnesotans covered

Murphy is a registered nurse. Before elected office, she ran the Minnesota Nurses Association as executive director. Her legislative record reflects that background directly.

In 2025, she championed the Keeping Nurses at the Bedside Act, a bill targeting hospital short-staffing and retention. She led Senate DFL efforts to protect health coverage for Minnesotans as federal cuts threatened Medicaid and other programs.

When Trump’s “Big Beautiful Bill” threatened healthcare funding, Murphy was front and center in opposition—not as a press statement, but as majority leader setting the Senate’s response agenda.3

The surplus that was spent

The deficit she now manages

By 2025, MMB projected a structural deficit of $2.96–$6 billion by 2028–29. Murphy now leads Senate Democrats in proposing up to $2.5 billion in cuts to respond to a gap that emerged after the 2023–25 governing period.

CBS Minnesota, MMB November forecast: "previously projected $6 billion budget deficit for 2028." Reformer: Murphy caucus proposes "nearly $2.5 billion in cuts."6,7

What that means for lawmakers.
