The Record

A Faribault Project
with a Bill Number

SF 1087 did one specific thing. It proposed $500,000 in Minnesota bond proceeds for the City of Faribault and the River Bend Nature Center. Site preparation. A new visitor and education facility. Public outdoor space. Trail improvements.1

Jasinski chief-authored the bill in the 2025 session. A companion House bill, HF 578, was heard in the House Capital Investment Committee with testimony from River Bend's executive director. The money goes to a place people can walk to, take kids to, and use without paying.3

River Bend sits on 743 acres along the Straight River in Faribault. It opened to the public in 1980 after a decade of community organizing. It serves schools, families, and working Minnesotans who need green space that asks nothing of them at the gate.2

District 19 Profile

The district covers communities across Goodhue, Rice, Steele, and Waseca counties. The economy runs on agriculture, food processing, manufacturing, logistics, and health care. Farmland here is among the most productive in the country.6

Analysis

What a Pattern Means

A single enforcement agreement with the Campaign Finance Board can be explained. Filings are complex. Rules shift. Committees make mistakes. The CFB's process exists to correct them, and a first offense resolved by agreement is, taken alone, a minor paper-trail fact.

A second enforcement agreement in the same category is different. It means the first resolution did not produce lasting change. It means the committee returned to the same compliance territory six years later and needed the board's intervention again. That is not a filing mistake. That is a compliance posture.

For a senator who runs on competence, growth-minded governance, and civic reliability — for a Deputy Minority Leader who presents himself as an administrator of the public trust — the question is simple. If you cannot run a clean committee, what does that say about how you run everything else?
